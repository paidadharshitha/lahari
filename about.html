Adv. Lahari Gadamsetty

Adv. Lahari Gadamsetty is a dedicated advocate based in Hyderabad, Telangana, with a profound commitment to the pursuit of justice. With years of rigorous practice across diverse areas of law, she has built a reputation for meticulous legal analysis, compassionate client advocacy, and unwavering ethical standards.

She holds a comprehensive legal qualification and is enrolled with the Bar Council, bringing a disciplined and scholarly approach to every case she undertakes. Her practice spans civil litigation, land disputes, loan recovery, insolvency proceedings, consumer protection, family law, cybercrime, and corporate advisory services.

Adv. Gadamsetty is deeply rooted in the belief that every individual deserves access to competent and principled legal representation. She combines her academic foundation with practical courtroom experience, ensuring that clients receive counsel that is both strategically sound and personally attentive.

Our Firm

Lahari Legal Associates is founded on the pillars of integrity, diligence, and an unwavering resolve to serve the interests of our clients with distinction.

Established in Hyderabad, Lahari Legal Associates has grown into a trusted name in the legal community of Telangana. The firm was conceived with a singular vision — to bridge the gap between complex legal challenges and effective, accessible solutions for individuals and businesses alike.

Our approach is grounded in thorough legal research, transparent communication, and a relentless focus on outcomes. We understand that legal matters can be deeply personal and often daunting; therefore, we prioritize building relationships of trust with every client who entrusts us with their case.

From intricate property disputes and insolvency matters to sensitive family law cases and emerging cybercrime challenges, our firm offers a breadth of expertise under one roof. Each matter receives the individual attention it deserves, backed by the collective knowledge and resources of our dedicated practice.
