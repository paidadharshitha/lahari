Land Disputes

Resolving boundary conflicts, title discrepancies, survey irregularities, and agricultural land disputes across Telangana.

Overview

Land disputes are among the most complex and emotionally charged legal matters in India. Whether you are confronting an encroachment on your ancestral property, grappling with conflicting survey records, or challenging a fraudulent title transfer, Lahari Legal Associates brings decades of combined experience navigating the nuances of Telangana's land revenue laws. Our team meticulously examines cadastral maps, revenue records, and registration documents to build airtight cases that protect your rightful ownership.

From agricultural land in rural districts to urban plots in rapidly developing corridors of Hyderabad, we handle disputes arising out of partition deeds, gift settlements, government acquisitions, and adverse possession claims. We work closely with licensed surveyors, revenue department officials, and expert witnesses to ensure every angle of your case is thoroughly investigated and forcefully presented before civil courts, tribunals, and revenue authorities.

Our Process

1

Title & Record Verification

We begin by conducting a comprehensive title search across the Sub-Registrar's office, Tahsildar records, and digital land registries to establish a clear chain of ownership and identify any encumbrances or irregularities.

2

Survey & Site Inspection

Our team coordinates with government-approved surveyors to conduct fresh measurements, compare boundary markers, and document the current physical possession status of the disputed land parcel.

3

Legal Strategy & Filing

Based on our findings, we draft and file the appropriate civil suit — whether a title suit under the Specific Relief Act, a partition suit, a declaratory suit, or an application before the Revenue Divisional Officer — tailored to the specifics of your dispute.

4

Representation & Resolution

We represent you through every hearing, negotiate settlement opportunities where beneficial, and pursue the case through appellate stages if necessary to secure a favourable final decree protecting your land rights.
