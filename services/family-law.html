Overview

Family law matters are among the most emotionally charged legal issues anyone can face. Whether you are navigating a difficult divorce, fighting for child custody, seeking maintenance, or looking to adopt a child, the decisions made during these proceedings have lifelong implications for you and your loved ones. At Lahari Legal Associates, our family law practice combines legal precision with genuine empathy, ensuring that your rights are protected while minimizing the emotional toll on all parties involved—especially children.

Our family law attorneys are well-versed in the Hindu Marriage Act, Special Marriage Act, Guardian and Wards Act, and other personal laws applicable to diverse communities in India. We handle contested and mutual consent divorces, child custody and visitation disputes, alimony and maintenance claims, domestic violence cases under the Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, and adoption proceedings. Our goal is always to seek amicable resolutions through mediation and counseling, but we are fully prepared to litigate aggressively when necessary to protect your family's well-being and financial security.

Our Process

1

Confidential Case Assessment

We begin with a private consultation to understand your situation, explain your legal rights under applicable personal law, and outline realistic outcomes based on the specifics of your family circumstances.

2

Mediation & Settlement Attempts

Before proceeding to court, we facilitate structured mediation sessions with the opposing party to reach mutually acceptable terms on custody, alimony, and asset division, reducing conflict and court time.

3

Petition Filing & Court Proceedings

When settlement is not possible, we file the appropriate petition—whether for divorce, custody, maintenance, or adoption—and represent you through all hearings, presenting evidence and legal arguments before the family court.

4

Decree Enforcement & Modification

After obtaining court orders, we assist with enforcement of custody decrees, maintenance payments, and alimony. We also handle modification petitions when circumstances change significantly.
