Money Recovery

Recovering outstanding debts, dishonoured cheques, loan defaults, and disputed payments through legal proceedings and arbitration.

Home > Services > Money Recovery

Overview

Outstanding receivables and unpaid debts can severely strain business operations and personal finances alike. Whether you are a supplier awaiting payment from a chronic defaulter, an individual recovering a personal loan, or a business pursuing dishonoured cheques under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act, Lahari Legal Associates provides aggressive and systematic money recovery services designed to convert legal claims into actual recoveries. Our approach combines the deterrent power of legal notices with the enforceability of court decrees and arbitration awards.

We understand that time is of the essence in debt recovery — every day of delay diminishes the likelihood of successful collection. Our team initiates proceedings under the most appropriate legal framework, whether that means a summary suit under Order XXXVII of the Civil Procedure Code for clear-cut debt cases, a Section 138 NI Act prosecution for bounced cheques, or commercial arbitration where the contract provides for it. We also assist with insolvency and bankruptcy proceedings under the IBC Code when the debtor's financial situation demands a more aggressive recovery strategy.

Our Process

1

Debt Verification & Documentation Review

We thoroughly review all loan agreements, promissory notes, invoices, bank statements, email correspondence, and cheque records to verify the debt amount, establish a clear paper trail, and identify the strongest legal basis for recovery.

2

Legal Notice & Pre-Litigation Demand

We issue a formal legal demand notice specifying the outstanding amount with interest and applicable legal costs, giving the debtor a defined window to settle — this often compels payment without the need for prolonged litigation.

3

Filing Suit or Arbitration

Where the debtor fails to comply, we file the appropriate legal action — a money recovery suit, a Section 138 cheque bounce complaint, or initiate arbitration proceedings — and obtain interim orders for attachment of property or bank accounts where feasible.

4

Execution & Enforcement

Once a decree or award is obtained, we pursue aggressive execution through the court — including garnishee orders, property auctions, and debtor examination — ensuring the judgement translates into actual money in your hands.
